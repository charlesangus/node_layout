_center_x() helper (added in plan 02-02):
```python
def _center_x(child_width, parent_x, parent_width):
    """Return the xpos that centers a child tile over a parent tile."""
    return parent_x + (parent_width - child_width) // 2
```

Consumer position in place_subtree(): `x` is the consumer's xpos (set via `node.setXpos(x)` at the top of the function), `node.screenWidth()` is the consumer tile width.

Decision from CONTEXT.md:
- After recursing into a diamond Dot, detect it by its custom knob and move JUST the Dot node to consumer-center-x.
- The Dot's own upstream inputs are unaffected (they remain at their recursed positions).
- Formula: dot_x = _center_x(dot.screenWidth(), x, node.screenWidth())
           = x + (node.screenWidth() - dot.screenWidth()) // 2
